Project Summary – Xscape: The Equation Quest 
Spectrum 2025 – Maths Fest (Activity 3: Number Knockout) 
By: Jagrat Ahuja and Arnav Singh 
Class: XI-A 
School: Shah International School 
🎮 Game Title: Xscape: The Equation Quest 
📌 Objective: 
Xscape: The Equation Quest is a browser-based math game designed to strengthen concepts of linear 
equations, inequalities, and word problems in a fun, engaging, and team-oriented way. It is intended for 
students from Classes IX–XII, with a focus on collaborative learning through gamified practice. 
Key Features: 
  10-Level Progression System: 
o  Levels 1, 3, 5, 7: Linear equations 
o  Levels 2, 4, 6, 8: Inequalities (players solve for the smallest positive integer) 
o  Levels 9–10: Easy Word problems to ensure a class 9 student can solve it 
  Pass-and-Play Multiplayer Format: 
Promotes teamwork and peer learning as players take turns solving math problems. 
  Randomized Questions: 
Each level draws 5 questions randomly from a pool of 30 per category. 
  Scoring and Progression: 
Players must score 3 or more out of 5 to unlock the next level. 
  End Credits Scene: 
Includes scrolling acknowledgments and a final “Thank You” screen after Level 10. 
Visual and UI Design: 
  Level scenes are uniquely themed 
  Lock icons indicate locked levels on the map screen. 
  Dedicated instruction screen and clear navigation buttons for smooth UX. 
  Includes mute/unmute functionality for background music and button click SFX. 
Audio Features: 
  Background Music: Calm, copyright-free music that loops across levels. 
  Sound Effects: Button click sound effect added for user feedback. 
Technologies & Tools Used: 
  Engine: Unity (6.1) 
  Languages: C# (MonoBehaviour scripting) 
  Design: Photoshop (for graphics), Unity UI System 
  Audio: Royalty-free music and sound effects (YouTube Audio Library) 
  Hosting: Itch.io (HTML5 WebGL build), Unity Publishing Software 
Educational Value: 
  Reinforces core algebra skills in a non-intimidating, engaging format. 
  Encourages critical thinking, collaboration, and mental math. 
  Suitable for classroom use, peer learning, or self-practice.
